Alpha Conde: Guinea President Put Under Arrest as Military Takes Over Gov't

July 28, 2024
Post
  • The military plotted a takeover in Conakry that turned out successful on Sunday, September 5, seeing the detention of President Alpha Conde
  • The officials accuse Conde of overseeing corruption in government and plunging the country to almost irredeemable levels of poverty
  • However, the act was condemned by the UN secretary-general Antonio Guterres who called for the immediate release of the besieged president

Guinea's President Alpha Conde is reported to be detained after the military took over the reins in Conakry.

Earlier on Sunday, September 5, amateur videos made rounds on social media showing a military official confirming the arrest of Conde who has been at the helm of the West African country for over a decade now.

The takeover was later confirmed after the Head of Guinea’s Special Forces Group (GFS), Mamady Doumbouya, appeared on the national broadcaster to confirm the coup, with Conde having been arrested.

The 83-year-old is accused of plummeting the country into poverty and overseeing wanton corruption in the government, the reason the military plotted the coup.

After the seizure of power, the military has vowed to rewrite a new constitution to set new dawn for the nation.

Conde took power in 2010 after spending many years in the opposition.

The United Nations secretary-general Antonio Guterres condemned the act, calling on the officers to release the president who has been put under arrest since the forced takeover.

"I am personally following the situation in Guinea very closely. I strongly condemn any takeover of the government by force of the gun and call for the immediate release of President Alpha Conde," said Guterres on Twitter.

The Africa Union (AU) also condemned the events in Conakry, summoning the umbrella's security council to examine the situation before recommending the necessary actions.

"The current President of the African Union H.E. Félix Tshisekedi and the Chairman of the African Union Commission H.E. Moussa Faki Mahamat condemn any seizure of power by force and call for the immediate release of President Alpha Conde," read a communique released by the AU."They invite the Peace and Security Council of the African Union to meet urgently to examine the new situation in Guinea and to take appropriate measures in the circumstances," the release read on.

The opposition took to the streets to celebrate.

They said they had suffered for years under Conde's rule, the takeover now becoming a lifeline for them as they anticipate a better administration.

The president is said to be unharmed though his whereabouts remain a mystery.
