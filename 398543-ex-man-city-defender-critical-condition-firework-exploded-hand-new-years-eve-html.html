Ex Man City defender in critical condition after firework exploded in his hand on New Years Eve

Former Manchester City defender Omar Elabdellaoui was on Thursday rushed to hospital after a freak accident saw a firework detonate on his face.

Elabdellaoui, who now plays for Turkish club Galatasaray is in critical condition and there are fears the incident could leave him partially blind.

According to reports from the Sun, the 29-year old ushered the new year at his home at the time of the accident.

The accident reportedly left him with burns on his face and some damage to his eye.

Galatasaray football club has since released a statement describing the defender’s condition as ‘life-threatening’.

"Our player Omar Elabdellaoui, who was taken to our sponsor hospital Liv Hospital after an unfortunate accident at his home in the evening, was given the first intervention."The damage to the eyes of our player who has burns on his face will be understood after further examinations,” a statement from the club read.

Further reports indicate the club’s vice president Abdurrahim Albayrak, coach Fatih Terim and captain Arda Turan visited Elabdellaoui in hospital.

Galatasaray assured fans they would issue another update on the defender’s condition as soon as possible.

Elabdellaoui has been an integral part of both his club and the Norway national side over the past few months.

He has featured 20 times for both club and country this season and played in Norway’s Nations League victories against Romania and Northern Ireland in October 2020.
