He was approached by two employees working for an airline and when he was asked for identification, he allegedly showed them an airport ID badge that had been reported missing since October 26.

The employees called 911 and Singh was taken into custody. He was charged with criminal trespass, a felony, theft and a misdemeanour, according to Chicago Tribune

In court, the assistant state attorney Kathleen Hagerty told the judge that Singh was been given food by other passengers and had also found the badge at the airport hence kept it.

The 36-year-old who has a master's degree in hospitality is reported to have no criminal background but the judge, Susana Ortiz, found the case appalling and that Singh's actions made him a danger to the community.
