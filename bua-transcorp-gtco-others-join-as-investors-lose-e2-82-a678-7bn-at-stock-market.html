BUA, Transcorp, GTCO, Others Join As Investors Lose 78.7bn At Stock Market

April 02, 2024
Post

Investors recorded more loss at the Nigeria stock market on Tuesday, losing the sum of N78.7 billion.

The loss is a follow-up to Monday’s decline in trading, Naija News understands.

Tuesday records show that there was a dip in equity capitalisation by 0.30 per cent at the close of the day’s business.

The slide brought down the equity capitalisation from N25.43 trillion recorded on Monday to N25.35 trillion today.

The development affected the likes of BUA Foods which shed N2.60kobo from its market price, dropping from N64.40kobo to N61.80kobo per share.

Similarly, the All-Share Index was down by 146.15 basis points to close at 47, 057.24 compared to 47,203.39 posted the previous day.

The decline was also reflected in the volume of trade with investors trading 238.21 million shares valued at N5.08 billion in 4,826 deals on Tuesday.

This was lower than 285.47 million shares worth N5.12 billion that exchanged hands in 5,342 deals on Monday.

Transcorp had sold 21.39 million shares worth N24.84 million, while NGX traded 17.23 million valued at N426.63 million, Naija News reports.

Presco led the gainers’ chart after its share was appreciated by N9.50kobo and rose from N95 per share to N104.50kobo.

NEM gained N0.30kobo to end trading with N3.31kobo from N3.01 per share.

Ikeja Hotel’s share was up by N0.13kobo to move from N1.33kobo to N1.46kobo per share.

Sharing in the huge slide, Fidelity Bank traded 22.29 million shares valued at N63.93 million.

Sunu Assurance share price rose by 9.09 per cent to move from N0.33kobo to N0.36kobo per share.

AIICO gained 5.80 per cent to end trading with N0.73kobo from N0.69kobo per share.

Courtville led the losers’ chart after shedding 7.27 per cent from its share to drop from N0.55kobo to N0.51kobo per share.

UPDC’s share depreciated from N1.06 to N1 per share after losing 5.66 per cent during trading.

UPL’s share price dropped by N0.14kobo to end trading at N2.45kobo from N2.59kobo per share.

Unilever lost N0.60kobo to end trading with N13.15kobo from N13.75kobo per share.

GTCO led the day’s trading with 32.35 million shares worth N887.63 million.

Zenith Bank traded 24.32 million valued at N653.46 million.
