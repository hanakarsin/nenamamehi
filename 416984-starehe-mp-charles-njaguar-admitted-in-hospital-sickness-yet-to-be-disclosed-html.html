Starehe MP Charles Njagua Undergoes Successful Knee Surgery

July 09, 2024
Post
  • MP and singer Jaguar underwent corrective surgery on Monday, June 28
  • The MP had injured his left foot in 2019 and since then, he has been in constant pains
  • His situation worsened when he returned from India a few weeks ago, medics recommending surgery to stop the pain

Starehe Member of Parliament Charles Njagua alias Jaguar was on Monday, June 28, admitted to MP Shah Hospital in Nairobi after he experienced pains in his left knee.

An amateur video of the legislator being moved to a ward while on stretcher emerged on Monday, June 28, sparking speculation from netizens.

The clip was shared by gospel artiste DK Kwenye Beat, who said Jaguar has been experiencing bouts of pain on his left foot for the past two years.

“He underwent surgery today, Monday at around 5am at the MP Shah Hospital, his right knee was operated on. He has been experiencing pains on the foot for while now." DK told Citizen.

The pains reportedly worsened a fortnight ago after he returned from India before surgery was recommended.

Jaguar is said to have injured his right foot when he engaged members of the city council inspectorate in a melee after the latter troubled traders at Muthurwa, Marikiti and Wakulima markets.

The city council askaris conducted an operation to eject traders who had allegedly encroached on the road. Jaguar assembled the traders to address them when police started to lob teargas on them.

The lawmaker injured himself as he tried to run from the police.

“As I was addressing the traders, the police threw teargas at us and that’s when I got injured as I was trying to run away from the tear gas," Jaguar said then.“I did not even realise that I had broken my leg at the moment, I later noticed that my leg had swollen,” he added.

Last year, the MP was admitted to a hospital in Nairobi.

In a video seen by TUKO.co.ke and which was posted on his Instagram page, the lawmaker was undergoing a CT scan but did not say much.

Captioning the video, the Starehe MP who also doubles as a musician said he was tired of being sick.

"Sick and tired of feeling sick and tired," wrote Jaguar.

Incitement allegations

In 2019, Jaguar was arrested following alleged xenophobic statements he made against foreigners doing business in Kenya.

DCI detectives nabbed the MP on Wednesday, June 26, who pounced on him outside the National Assembly.

He was later taken to the Directorate of Criminal Investigation (DCI) headquarters along Kiambu Road, where he was expected to record a statement.

Jaguar, as he is popularly known and who led local traders to protest the dominance of foreigners in Kenyan markets, said he would personally flush out the traders within 24-hours if the government failed to heed his call.
