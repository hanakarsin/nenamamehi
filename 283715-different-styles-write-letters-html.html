Different styles used to write letters

March 17, 2024
Post

Excellent letter writing requires a person to know the various styles used to write letters. Knowing when to apply them is also essential. Notably, the manner in which a letter is written determines the impression that the receiver will create about the sender. When you send a letter to a business partner, a potential employer, a workmate, a colleague or an investor, you not only deliver the message but also tell them about you. Because it is important to create a good first impression, it is wise to know how to write and also when to apply the styles used to write letters.

There are majorly three acceptable business letter styles. These include block, modified block and semi-block formats.

Different letter formats

Outlined below are the different styles of letter writing:

1. Full block style business letter format

In this type of letter format, all the elements are usually aligned to the left side of the paper. Generally, this format is very simple and many people prefer it because it appears neat. When using it, you separate the paragraphs by double spacing. Because of a formal appearance, the full block style letter format is used in formal business circumstances. However, it is a very easy format and many people still use it even during informal business situations. This format is advantageous because it works well regardless of the formality of the situation.

You start by writing details about you or your business. These include your name, your address, your phone number and the date of the day you are sending the letter. In the next paragraph, write the details of the recipient. These include their name, company’s name and address. Proceed to write the message while ensuring that there is double line spacing between the sentences.

The simplicity in this letter is brought about by the fact that all the elements are aligned on the left side. The only difference between the full block format and the semi-blocked format is that in the former, the words are not indented.

In some cases, you might want to include some enclosures in the letter. To do this, type the word ‘enclosures’ immediately after the signature block. You can then proceed to list some of the things you have attached such as an invoice, a brochure or any other item.

2. Semi block style letter

Unlike the full block letter format, the semi block style is a little less formal. However, it can be used during different situations, whether formal or informal. Just like the name suggests, it is not fully blocked. All the elements are aligned to the left side of the paper apart from the beginning of paragraphs which are indented five spaces. The other difference is that the ending words (your last name and title should appear on the right margin of paper). But the enclosure’s part should remain on the right side. The paragraphs are usually spaced using a double line spacing format. This format is also known as the indented style letter480/mo.

3. Modified block letter format

Amongst the 3 different types of letter styles, the modified block letter format is the most informal. This can be used for formal purpose. Also, it appears to be a bit more complex than the other two formats. When writing, you start by writing your personal details, name and address on the right side of the paper. You should then skip a line and write the date (DD/MM/YY) which should still right-aligned. Then on your left side write the details of the recipient, name, address and phone number (not a must).

In the body of the letter, all the information must be aligned to the left side just like in the full block letter style. The last part should be written on the right side. If there are some enclosures include them at the bottom left part of the paper. Do not forget to include the number of items.

What to note with all types of letter formats

Aforementioned, a letter speaks more about an individual or a business. Therefore, using the correct formats excellently can help one create a good impression which can translate to increased sales for a business. Other things you should pay attention to while writing the letters include;

1. Observe the fonts, spacing and margins

To make your letter easy to read and formal, pay attention to your font. Ensure that the font is either 12-point Times New Roman or Arial. All around the paper leave a margin of 1-inch. This enables the letter to have a professional touch. Leaving an even margin also makes your letter look organized and very easy for the recipient to go through it.

If you are suing the full block style, ensure to skip a line after the return address. In the other formats you do not necessarily have to skip a line.

Spacing is also crucial in the appearance of the letter. Always use a double spacing. After every section of the letter, skip a line.

2. Sections

All business letters have various sections regardless of the format that you use. You begin with the heading which contains the return address, the date and the recipient’s address. The next section is the salutation. If you know the name of the recipient you can write “Dear Mrs. Harvey”. If you don’t know the name you should write, (Dear Sir/Madam). The next sections include the body, the closing and the signature line. However, you might want to indicate that there some enclosures which you should include on the left bottom part of the letter.

When closing the letter, the word sincerely should be followed by a comma. You should then skip four lines, sign and then type your name and title.

3. Consistency

Consistency is key when writing a letter. Avoid underlining, italicizing or bolding your words unless required. When you do this, you make the letter seem unprofessional. Also, space your paragraphs in the body. Writing a whole block of information can make it hard for the receiver of the letter to follow through. Sometimes, they might get bored of reading the whole of it.

4. Avoid errors

Small grammatical errors and poor sentence structures can render the letter unimpressive or unprofessional. You should not misspell words or use slang language. Use the correct punctuation marks. As such, ensure to read through your letter to ensure that the correct vocabulary and sentence structures have been used. You can even ask someone else to proof read it for you before you send it.

Go direct to the point. Usually, letters should not be very long. Just explain yourself or the situation. In case you want to add other details, list them in the enclosure segment at the left bottom part of the paper.
