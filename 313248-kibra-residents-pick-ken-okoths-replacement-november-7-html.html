Kibra residents to pick Ken Okoth's replacement on November 7

May 15, 2024
Post

- Speaker Justin Muturi on Wednesday, August 14, declared Kibra MP seat vacant and wrote to the poll body to set a by-election date

- IEBC urged political parties interested in the race to submit aspirant's names and conduct primaries not later than Monday, August 26

- Already, at least six candidates among them Ken Okoth's brother Imran have expressed interest in succeeding the fallen MP

- Campaigns ahead of the by-election are set to kick off on Monday, September 9, 2019 and cease on Monday, November 4

The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) has set Thursday, November 7, 2019 as the date for Kibra Constituency MP by-election.

This is after Speaker Justin Muturi declared the MP seat vacant following death of Ken Okoth on Friday, July 26.

TUKO.co.ke has learnt that already six aspirants among them Okoth's brother Imran have already showed interest in clinching the seat.

"Following the declaration of vacancy in the office of member of the National Assembly for Kibra constituency vide a letter from the National Assembly speaker received on Wednesday, August 14, there shall be a by-election for Kibra constituency on Thursday, November 7, 2019," read gazette notice number 7378.

Political parties intending to participate in the by-election were urged to submit names of those contesting in party primaries and the dates, which should be on or before Monday, August 26.

This came amid uncertainty on whether or not Jubilee party will field a candidate in the ODM predominant constituency.

The party bowed out of Ugenya and Embakasi South by-elections in favour of the Orange party, a decision it attributed to the truce between President Uhuru Kenyatta and Opposition leader Raila Odinga.

Party Secretary General Raphael Tuju said Jubilee was still holding consultative meetings and will announce its decision soon on the Kibra mini poll.

"We are yet to make a decision on the matter we are still consulting before we can issue the official position of the party," Tuju was quoted by the Standard on Monday, Augusts 19..
