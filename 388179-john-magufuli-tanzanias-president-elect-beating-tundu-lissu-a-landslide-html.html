John Magufuli is Tanzania's president elect after beating Tundu Lissu with a landslide

February 17, 2024
Post

- President John Magufuli has won re-election with a landslide victory after garnering 12.5 million votes

- His competitor, Tundu Lissu of the CHADEMA party garnered 1-9 million votes describing the poll as fraudulent

- He claimed his agents were prevented from accessing polling station during the election

Tanzania President John Magufuli was on Friday, October 30, declared the winner of the presidential election by the National Electoral Commission (NEC).

.Magufuli of Chama Cha Mapinduzi (CCM) received 12.5 million votes (85%) while his main challenger, Tundu Lissu of the Chadema party got 1.9 million votes (13%), the commission said.

Magufuli's competitor, Lissu who came in second dismissed the election results saying the exercise was marred with widespread irregularities," The BBC reported.

“The commission declares John Magufuli of CCM who garnered the majority of votes as the winner in the presidential seat,” said commission chairman Semistocles Kaijage.

Lissu claimed his agents were prevented from accessing polling station during the Wednesday, October 28, election which he termed as a travesty but NEC dismissed the claims of fraud.

Magufuli who was seeking a second five-year term had promised voters he would boost the economy by completing ambitious projects he had started in his first term

An assessment released by Tanzania Election Watch which is a group of regional experts indicated the vote marked the most significant backsliding in Tanzania’s democratic credentials.

It noted there was a heavy deployment of military and police officers who presence and conduct created a climate of fear.

“The electoral process, so far, falls way below the acceptable international standards” for holding free and fair elections, the group said.

Few international observers were allowed to watch the vote.

The US assistant secretary of state for African affairs, Tibor Nagy, said on Friday, October 30 they were deeply concerned about reports of systematic interference in the democratic process.

“We continue to review credible allegations of the use of force against unarmed civilians,” he said in a tweet.

The head of NEC Semistocles Kaijage said allegations of fake ballot papers were unsubstantiated.

TUKO.co.ke previously reported as well as taking part in the Tanzanian elections, voters on the semi-autonomous island group of Zanzibar were also electing their own president.

The CCM candidate Hussein Mwinyi was declared the winner with 76% of the votes.

His main rival, Maalim Seif Sharif of ACT-Wazalendo, got 19% - his biggest defeat in any presidential election he has taken part in.

On Thursday, October 29, Seif was arrested shortly after saying that the polls had been rigged and calling for mass protests. He was later released on bail.
