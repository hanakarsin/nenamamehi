Family of slain ex-University of Nairobi students' leader Samuel Ragira has urged the government to speed up investigations and bring to book his killers.

“Our parents sacrificed so much to bring us up. Death has robbed us of a child who was the shining light in our family. To those who killed Sammy, I know you too have children and God will deal with you,” she sobbed.

Ragira was shot dead by goons at Klabuu market in Nairobi after he confronted his attackers on the night of Thursday, March 13.

A day before meeting his death, the late Nairobi politician had claimed on Facebook alleged Mungiki members were demanding traders pay KSh 10,000 each in exchange for stalls.

"It is unfortunate that Mungiki are terrorising Klabu 36 traders with pangas and guns taking advantage of the situation. They are demanding KSh 10,000 from every trader to build a kibanda," he posted.
