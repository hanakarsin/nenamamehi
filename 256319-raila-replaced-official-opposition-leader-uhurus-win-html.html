Raila replaced as official opposition leader after Uhuru's win

April 26, 2024
Post

- NASA leader Raila Odinga's role as the opposition leader was assumed by Thirdway Alliance's Ekuru Aukot who came second in the repeat election

- Raila boycotted the repeat election and termed it a sham

- Uhuru Kenyatta was declared winner on Monday in the most divisive election ever held in Kenya

- Aukot has already started playing his role as he wants President-elect Uhuru Kenyatta to start the process of uniting the country

For the first time since 2007, Kenya will have a new opposition leader after NASA leader Raila Odinga's role was taken up following Uhuru Kenyatta's win in the repeat poll.

Raila boycotted the fresh election held on Thursday, October 26. He said IEBC as constituted was incapable of conducting a free, fair and credible election.

The former premier was Uhuru's main challenger in the election and his withdrawal meant another candidate would assume his role.

Thirdway Alliance's Ekuru Aukot came second in the election which Uhuru won by 98%. Aukot automatically assumed the role of opposition leader.

Aukot conceded to Uhuru and his first role as opposition leader was to call on the president elect to start the process of uniting Kenyans.

“Today we take our role as the official opposition and to president-elect Uhuru Kenyatta congratulations, we shall put your government into check,” Aukot said through Twitter.

As reported by TUKO.co.ke, Raila (an opposition leader for over a decade) is set to announce his next cause of action on Tuesday, October 31.

He has already dismissed Uhuru's win, saying the NASA does not recognise him as president of Kenya.

Raila has also called on his supporters to embrace civil disobedience and non-cooperation as a way of teaching Uhuru a lesson.
