Ahmednasir Supports William Ruto's Blockage from Travelling to Uganda: &quot;His Votes are in Kenya&quot;

June 14, 2024
Post
  • Senior counsel Ahmednasir Abdullahi said he was in agreement with the order that stopped William Ruto from flying to Uganda
  • Ahmednasir said Ruto should stay in Kenya and campaign for his 2022 presidential bid since his term as DP is coming to an end
  • The deputy president was barred from travelling to Uganda on Monday, August 2, over clearance issues

City lawyer Ahmednasir Abdullahi has supported the move by immigration officers to block Deputy President William Ruto from travelling to Uganda.

The deputy president was prevented from leaving the country on Monday, August 2, after spending hours at the Wilson Airport due to lack of clearance.

In a statement on his Twitter, the senior counsel said Ruto should stay in Kenya and concentrate on his 2022 presidential campaigns since he has less than a year in office as the deputy president.

"Kenyans have forced me to comment on Uhuru's injunction stopping DP Ruto from "Tangatanging" in UG and his orders that Ruto stays in Kenya to concentrate on his 2022 presidential campaign. On this, I agree with Uhuru 100%. Ruto's votes are in Kenya and he has just 11 months as DP!" he said.

Ruto's reaction

As reported, in a simple tweet sent on Monday evening, Ruto seemed to have shaken hands to the fate that the injunction had brought.

"Isorait...Tumwachie Mungu. (It's alright, let's leave it to God)," he said.

According to reports, the deputy president needed to be authorised by the Head of Public Service before boarding a flight to Kenya's western neighbour.

The trip was halted following a sudden change of protocol after the immigration department was tasked to clear him.

Stranded at Wilson Airport

Ruto's travel arrangements had initially been handled by officials assigned to his office.

Accompanying Ruto was Kiharu MP Ndindi Nyoro and his Kapseret counterpart Oscar Sudi.

According to Sudi, the deputy president was stranded at Wilson Airport since midday and, out of impatience, he left the airport for his home.

The two legislators had been cleared but could not figure out the reason behind the delay in clearing the second in command.

Last month, the deputy president was in Uganda to lay the foundation stone for at Dei Group factory facility. President Yoweri Museveni hosted him.
