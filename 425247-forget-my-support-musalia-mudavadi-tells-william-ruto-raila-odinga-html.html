Musalia Mudavadi Expresses Confidence He Will Win 2022 Presidency: &quot;Time Has Come&quot;

Amani National Congress (ANC) leader Musalia Mudavadi has reiterated he would go to the wire in the 2022 presidential polls.

The former vice president told Deputy President William Ruto and Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga to forget about his support, insisting he won't play second fiddle.

Speaking to Inooro Fm on Tuesday, September 7, Mudavadi stated he had the requisite experience to lead the country.

“I have experience and the ticket from my party to vie for the presidency in next year’s elections. Anything short of that is not within my making. We have played second to others in previous elections and time has come for us to now captain the ship,” he said.

Mudavadi expressed confidence he would win the presidency and form the next government to succeed the Jubilee administration.

"I have been in government for some time. I have held several ministerial positions before. This is the path of my political and Cabinet experience in matters of government and how things are run. The experience I have garnered now informs my decision to form the coming government next year.”

Mudavadi to pick running mate from Mt Kenya

Mudavadi's statement came a few days after ANC deputy party leader Ayub Savula said he would pick a running mate from the vote-rich Mt Kenya region.

The lawmaker, a close ally of the former seasoned politician, insisted Mudavadi is the best fit to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta, describing him as a humble man.

"I am telling you that the strategy Musalia has adopted to bring governors from Central Kenya on board; we have Nyeri governor Mutahi Kahiga, governor Francis Kimemia, governor Anne Waiguru and governor Lee Kinyanjui. We have held secret meetings with those governors and they are willing to marshal Mt Kenya in support of Musalia," Savula said.

Uhuru's pressure

Uhuru has reportedly chosen Raila as his preferred presidential candidate.

On several occasions, the president has been calling on the former NASA co-principals to unite ahead of the 2022 polls.

Mudavadi and Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka, however, vowed they won't rally behind the ODM leader again.
