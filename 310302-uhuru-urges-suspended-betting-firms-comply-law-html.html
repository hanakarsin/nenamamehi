Uhuru urges suspended betting firms to comply with law

March 06, 2024
Post

President Uhuru Kenyatta has urged betting firms to comply with tax regulation policy if they want to continue operating in the country.

Uhuru who was reacting to a recent move by the Betting Control and Licensing Board (BCLB) to suspend 27 betting firms said the crackdown will continue to ensure only companies that comply with the law are allowed to operate.

Speaking during the traditional wedding ceremony of Kirinyaga Governor Anne Waiguru and lawyer Kamotho Waiganjo on Saturday, July 13, the president asked betting firms owners to stop threats of taking the matter to court and instead comply with the regulations.

"The firms should stop threats that they will move to court. The government must get its share to fund activities that are beneficial to this country," he said.

The head of state clarified the suspension was not targeting specific operators but was being done objectively to ensure no one evades paying tax.

He noted the firms were good for the country's economy but insisted the benefit could only be fully harnessed if owners paid taxes responsibly.

"Those in the betting companies are our friends but we have to agree that the government must get its rightful share to build cultural centres and other developments," added Uhuru

The government recently ordered all betting firms to apply afresh for operating licences, starting July.

The move was aimed at netting firms that have been evading taxes despite reaping billions of shillings in profits.

On Thursday, July 11, BCLB asked clients of various betting firms who still had money on their accounts to withdraw it within 48 hours.

A day later, telecommunication company Safaricom blocked deposit transactions to paybill numbers of blacklisted betting firms.
