Moses Kuria Says Deputy President Position Not Negotiable for Mt Kenya: &quot;We Have the Votes&quot;

March 25, 2024
Post
  • According to Moses Kuria, Mt Kenya must reap big in the coming post-2022 government due to the region's numerical strength
  • He said if the region supports an outsider in the presidential vote, then the deputy president post must be occupied by one from Mt Kenya
  • He also stated that the region must produce 40 per cent of those holding state posts such as ministerial, ambassadorial and heads of parastatals

Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria has said the Mt Kenya region would not settle for any less in the government that succeeds President Uhuru Kenyatta's.

The outspoken legislator stated that as the vote-rich region decides to back an outsider in the presidency, the deputy president post must be reserved for the region.

"We have agreed on the position of the deputy president is not negotiable. Commensurate with our votes, 40 per cent of the votes in this country are from the Mt Kenya region," said Kuria.

40 per cent of state appointments

Besides the deputy president position, Kuria was also categorical on how state appointments should be conducted in the newly elected government post-2022.

Owing to Mt Kenya's presumed political capital, Kuria says the appointments must reflect the region's numerical strength.

"We want to have a proportional share of government, not favourable. If we have got 22 ministers, six to seven ministers should come from the Mt Kenya region. We want 40 per cent of all the PSs, parastatal CEOs, chairmen and board members to be from the region," stated Kuria.

"We want 40 per cent of ambassadorial positions to be from the Mt Kenya region. It is not a favour we are asking for, it is the minimum we deserve and it is our right," he said.

Kuria is a staunch ally of Deputy President William Ruto who has been making constant inroads to the mountain to marshal support for his 2022 presidential bid.

Ruto is yet to decide on who will deputise him despite the indications he would pick his running mate from the region.

Among those (perceived) with the upper hand is Kuria himself, who has refused to fold his Chama Cha Kazi (CCK) party to fully join the hustler movement on which Ruto intends to ride while seeking to succeed Uhuru.

Also believed to be on Ruto's bucket list regarding the deputy president post is Mathira MP Rigathi Gachagua, who has been at loggerheads with Kuria concerning the unification of the Mt Kenya team that supports Ruto.

While Gachagua has been calling for a united party, Kuria has held that it would be rational that the Mt Kenya region has its party with which it would be championing the interests of the residents.
