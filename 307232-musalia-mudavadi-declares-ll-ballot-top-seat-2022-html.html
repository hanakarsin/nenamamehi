Musalia Mudavadi declares he'll vie for presidency in 2022

February 10, 2024
Post

- The ANC party leader said he is done supporting other candidates for the presidency and playing second fiddle

- He said he will go for the top seat one more time so he can also get an opportunity to serve Kenyans

- Other leaders who have openly expressed interest in the 2022 race include William Ruto, Alfred Mutua, Moses Kuria and Moses Wetang'ula

Amani National Congress (ANC) party leader Musalia Mudavadi has once again declared his interest in the 2022 race to State House, adding to the already long list of presidential hopefuls.

Mudavadi who is not new to the high-stakes competition, having unsuccessfully vied for the presidency in 2013 after a temporary fallout with ODM leader Raila Odinga, is certain he will be the man to beat.

In a statement issued on Tuesday, June 4, the former deputy prime minister said he will not play second fiddle to anyone again after former Kakamega senator Bonny Khalwale suggested he should join Deputy President William Ruto as a running mate in 2022.

"I have always been a selfless leader, ready to support others. This led to my founding of NASA as a means to give the country a new leadership that would move the country forward. Come 2022 I have decided to throw my hat in the ring so as to get the opportunity to serve Kenyans," he tweeted.

The presidential race has so far attracted a number of candidates, with each believing they have what it takes to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta.

Ruto, the presumed Jubilee party candidate, is widely perceived as the front-runner in the next poll, with many, despite rift in the ruling party, still expecting Uhuru to endorse him as a way of returning the favour for the DP's unwavering support in 2013 and 2017.

At the same time, Wiper party leader Kalonzo Musyoka who has been facing rebellion in his own backyard cannot be ruled out yet as much as he has not come out to confirm he will be running for the presidency in 2022.

Opposition chief Raila had, however, dismissed reports he was planning to be on the ballot for the fifth time even after his elder brother Oburu Odinga proposed that he considers it one more time.

At the same time, Bungoma senator and Ford Kenya party leader Moses Wetang'ula has also declared interest in the State House race.

Other presidential hopefuls are Machakos governor and Maendeleo Chap Chap (MCC) leader Alfred Mutua who has been trying to reposition himself as the next kingpin in Ukambani region, and Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria.

Kuria even suggested Ruto, Raila and hardworking Makueni governor Kivutha Kibwana as his potential running mates should any of them consider the offer.
